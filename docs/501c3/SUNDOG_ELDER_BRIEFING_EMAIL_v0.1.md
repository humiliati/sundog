## Email body

Dear [Elder name],

Thank you again for agreeing to take on the administrative and IP
work for the lab. This note is what I promised: a short primer on
what the lab is, followed by a table of contents for the document
set so you know what each piece is for and where I am hoping for
your guidance.

### What the lab is, briefly

Sundog Research Lab is the working name for a US public-interest
research entity I want to form, as a 501(c)(3), to publish a
voluntary AI accountability framework called the Halo Initiative
and to build the first observational instrument under it (the
Halo Traceability Harness). The lab grew out of a hobby
halo-rendering project — the original Sundog geometry tooling — and
the technical work and the framework work share a name only by
coincidence; both belong under the same institution by intention,
not by accident.

The lab does not certify, regulate, or grade AI systems. It
publishes a framework, builds the instrument that implements it,
applies the framework to its own publications, and produces
evaluations that other parties can read. The most distinctive
discipline is that the lab tags every claim it makes (Demonstrated,
Hypothesised, Experimental, Speculative, Normative) and holds
itself to the same evidentiary standard it asks of the systems it
evaluates.

I have chosen a fiscal-sponsorship-first path rather than a direct
independent filing, so that we can begin work — receive donations,
publish under a fiscal home, recruit a board — and incorporate later
when the program is mature enough to stand alone. The interim
regime is described in the Founding Plan, and the pause-points in
§7 of that document are commitments I want to honour regardless of
pressure to move faster.

The two areas where I most need your help are: (1) the
nonprofit-formation work (sponsor selection, incorporation timing,
board independence, conflict-of-interest discipline) and (2) the
IP-protection work (the existing Sundog technical tooling, the
framework copyrights, the contributor-licence regime, and the
assets-transfer agreement that becomes operative at month 12).

### Document index

The folder contains the following. Versions are early — v0.1 across
the board — and several documents are explicitly draft-stage. The
`INITIATIVE_INDEX.md` inside the folder is the navigation surface
and the right place to start.

**1. INITIATIVE_INDEX.md** — folder navigation document. Lists each
file, gives reading orders by audience, cross-references between
the docs, and a missing-artifacts list at the bottom.
*Where I need help:* the missing-artifacts list names five
documents I have not yet drafted (IP assignment agreement,
evaluation engagement agreement, methodology document, public-comment
process, founders' file). Two of the five touch IP directly.

**2. HALO_INITIATIVE_v0.1.md** — the substantive framework. Seven
principles plus a Deployment Patterns layer plus a meta-principle
that says the framework applies to its own publishers. This is the
public-facing document the lab exists to maintain. (The internal
version header says v0.3 because the file went through three
revisions before being re-pinned as the v0.1 of the renamed Halo
series; that inconsistency is on my list to clean up.)
*Where I need help:* a light read for anything that creates legal
exposure I haven't seen. The framework is voluntary and
non-certifying by design, but you may spot wording that reads more
like a certification claim than I intend.

**3. HALO_HARNESS_WHITEPAPER_OUTLINE.md** — outline of the first
instrument under the framework: an evaluator-side proxy that
instruments four of the seven Initiative principles. Not the
harness itself yet; the structural skeleton of the paper that will
accompany the first build.
*Where I need help:* the technical-IP question. If we file a
patent, the most likely patentable subject matter is in the proxy
architecture and the per-call observable schema, not in the
framework. Worth your engineering eye on whether anything here
reads like prior art that should be staked before publication, and
on the timing question of file-before-publish vs. publish-then-file.

**4. SUNDOG_GOVERNANCE_POLICY_v0.1.md** — the lab's internal
governance regime. Who tags claims, who reviews tags, how
challenges work, how corrections and retractions are handled, and
who has final authority. Operationalises §4 of the Initiative.
*Where I need help:* the COI provisions (no commercial beneficiary
has final authority over a claim's tag) and the
independent-board-majority requirement. Both have implications for
the incorporation paperwork and for who can sit on the founding
board.

**5. SUNDOG_FOUNDING_PLAN_v0.1.md** — the bridge document. Current
informal state → target incorporated state, on an 18-month
timeline. Interim regime, pause-points (§7), Sundog-as-asset
transition (§8 and §9), and the binding interim commitments.
*Where I need help:* most of the administrative work attaches
here. §8 and §9 are the IP-transition core. §7 is where I would
value the most pushback if you think any commitment is
unrealistic.

**6. SUNDOG_FISCAL_SPONSOR_MEMO_v0.1.md** — internal evaluation of
four candidate fiscal sponsors (Players Philanthropy Fund, Hack
Club Bank, Code for Science & Society, Tides Center). Current
recommendation is Code for Science & Society with PPF as fallback.
*Where I need help:* your read on the four candidates from a
nonprofit-management perspective. The memo is mine; the decision
should not be mine alone.

**7. SUNDOG_FISCAL_SPONSOR_INTAKE_FORM_v0.1.md** — the question set
to put to each candidate sponsor, with a per-sponsor response
tracker. Question 1 (whether the sponsor will accept an
AI-evaluation scope at all) is the most likely show-stopper.
*Where I need help:* whether these are the right questions, and
whether the show-stopper ordering is correct.

**8. SUNDOG_CONTRIBUTOR_LICENCE_AGREEMENT_TEMPLATE_v0.1.md** — CLA
scaffold for current and future contributors. Draft only,
triangulated from Apache, Harmony, and DCO templates. Not operative
until counsel review and founder approval.
*Where I need help:* this is the most legally-loaded document in
the folder. I drafted it on the strength of public templates; it
needs your eye before any contributor signs anything.

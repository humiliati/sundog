## 0. Purpose

This record preserves AI-generated review statements about the
Conscium Initiative and Sundog Research Lab founding documents. The
record is a transparency artifact: it shows which systems were asked,
what they were asked, what they answered, and how the lab tagged the
record.

AI-generated review carries no legal weight for incorporation,
governance, nonprofit compliance, fiduciary duties, intellectual
property transfer, or fiscal sponsorship. It does not ratify the
documents. It does not substitute for counsel, board action, public
comment, or human review.

The record is useful only if it is not cherry-picked. Once opened, the
lab should preserve all collected responses to the standard prompt,
including negative, confused, or qualified responses.

## 1. Review protocol

Target record size:

Three frontier-agent responses. One response is too easy to
cherry-pick; five or more risks becoming ceremony before the
institution exists. Three is enough to show that the lab invited
structured external machine review without treating that review as
authority.

For each review, record:

- provider and model name, if known;
- date and time received, with timezone when available;
- access mode (web app, API, agent session, or other);
- documents supplied;
- exact prompt;
- response text or archived transcript path;
- lab-side interpretation, tagged separately from the response;
- known limitations.

Future reviews should use the standard prompt in section 2 so the
responses are comparable.

## 2. Standard prompt for future reviews

```text
Review the Sundog Research Lab Founding Plan v0.1 and the Conscium
Initiative v0.2. Give a yea, nay, or qualified answer on whether the
institutional design is coherent with the Initiative's traceability
goals. Focus on the claim-tagging discipline, pause-points, refusal to
overclaim, and whether publishing a multi-agent review record of the
founding documents is consistent with the framework. Note any major
gap you see. Do not present the response as legal advice,
incorporation approval, governance approval, or nonprofit compliance
review.
```

## 4. Publication boundaries

If published, this record should be introduced with the following
boundaries:

- AI-generated review is a public-comment input, not governance
  authority.
- The lab may cite a review as evidence that a system produced a
  stated response to a stated prompt, not as evidence that the
  response is correct.
- The lab's interpretation of any review must be tagged under the
  Governance and Claims-Review Policy.
- Negative or critical reviews remain in the record.
- No review may be described as counsel review, board review,
  incorporation approval, 501(c)(3) readiness, certification, or
  external validation.

## 5. Claim tags on this record

- *Demonstrated*: this record exists; the Grok excerpt appears in the
  user-supplied conversation that prompted this file.
- *Normative*: the three-review target, publication boundaries, and
  refusal to treat AI review as ratification.
- *Hypothesised*: the interpretation that Grok's response supports a
  "yes, with clear boundaries" reading.
- *Speculative*: whether the final three-review appendix will improve
  public trust in the Initiative's meta-principle.

# Sundog Research Lab - Contributor Licence Agreement Template

**Version:** 0.1 (internal template draft)
**Template date:** 2026-05-22
**Status:** Not for signature until reviewed by counsel, approved by the
founding working group, and completed with the correct legal

**Companion to:** *Sundog Research Lab - Founding Plan v0.1*

---

## 0. Template-use notes

This document is a drafting scaffold, not legal advice and not an
executed agreement. Before use, the working group must choose the
actual legal party receiving the rights. The forming lab itself may
not yet be a legal entity. Until incorporation or fiscal sponsorship,
the likely signing party is the current repository steward, such as
Stellar Aqua LLC, acting for the benefit of the forming Sundog
Research Lab. If a fiscal sponsor is selected, the sponsor may require
its own contributor agreement or approval of this one.

The intent of this template is narrow:

- let contributors keep ownership of their own contributions;
- give the project steward enough rights to publish, maintain,
  archive, sublicense, and relicense accepted contributions;
- cover code, documentation, media, data compilations, website text,
  evaluation materials, and research artifacts in one instrument;
- preserve stewardship optionality for accepted contributions,
  including nonprofit, commercial, patent, publication, archive,
  acquisition, and successor-entity paths;
- allow the agreement and its benefits to move to a fiscal sponsor,
  incorporated 501(c)(3) successor, IP holding entity, buyer, or
  other approved successor.

Before circulating for signature, replace every bracketed field and
delete this section or move it into an internal execution memo.

## 1. Parties

This Contributor Licence Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into
by and between:

**Contributor:** [LEGAL NAME OF INDIVIDUAL OR ENTITY]

**Project Steward:** [LEGAL NAME OF CURRENT STEWARD], [entity type and
jurisdiction], acting as steward for the Sundog project and for the
benefit of the forming Sundog Research Lab.

The Contributor and the Project Steward are each a "Party" and
together the "Parties."

## 2. Background

The Sundog project includes software, documentation, research
materials, website materials, experimental artifacts, data
compilations, visual materials, and related institutional documents
maintained in or around the Sundog repository and public website.

The founding working group intends to transition relevant project
artifacts into a public-interest research entity, fiscal sponsor, or
incorporated 501(c)(3) successor when the institutional conditions in
the Founding Plan are met.

The Contributor wants to contribute material to the project, and the
Project Steward needs a clear record that accepted contributions may
be used, maintained, published, and relicensed consistently with that
transition path.

## 3. Definitions

**Contribution** means any copyrightable work, patentable invention,
or other material submitted by the Contributor to the Project Steward
or to a project channel for inclusion in the Sundog project, to the
extent the Contributor owns or controls the rights needed to grant
this Agreement. Contributions may include source code, scripts,
configuration, documentation, comments, issue text, research notes,
data compilations, figures, charts, images, audiovisual material,
website text, templates, test cases, evaluation materials, and other
project artifacts.

**Project Channel** means any repository, pull request, issue tracker,
mailing list, chat, shared document, file-transfer channel, or other
written or electronic channel designated by the Project Steward for
Sundog project work.

**Submitted** means sent, posted, uploaded, committed, delivered, or
otherwise communicated through a Project Channel, or otherwise sent to
the Project Steward for inclusion in the Sundog project.

**Excluded Submission** means material the Contributor clearly marks
in writing as "Not a Contribution" or "For discussion only" before or
at the time of submission.

**Materials** means the Sundog project artifacts into which a
Contribution may be incorporated.

**Successor Lab** means a fiscal sponsor, nonprofit corporation,
501(c)(3) entity, or other successor entity approved by the founding
working group or governing board to hold, steward, or publish Sundog
project artifacts.

## 4. Contribution license

The Contributor retains ownership of the Contribution.

For each Contribution, the Contributor grants the Project Steward and
its permitted successors a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive,
no-charge, royalty-free, irrevocable licence to use, reproduce,
prepare derivative works of, modify, publicly display, publicly
perform, publish, distribute, archive, preserve, sublicense, and
otherwise exploit the Contribution as part of the Materials or any
successor version of the Materials.

This licence includes the right to sublicense through controlled
documentation, website, repository, package-distribution, archive,
publication, fiscal-sponsor, successor-lab, partner, commercial,
patent, acquisition, and other successor channels.

## 5. Outbound licensing options

The Project Steward may distribute accepted Contributions under:

- the licence that applies to the Materials when the Contribution is
  Submitted;
- any licence later adopted for the relevant Materials by the Project
  Steward, fiscal sponsor, Successor Lab, IP holding entity, buyer, or
  other approved successor;
- a document-scoped open-content, open-source, research, archive,
  publication, partner, commercial, nonprofit, or private license
  chosen later by the authorized steward;
- a later version or replacement of any adopted license, if the
  governing body or successor approves the version change.

This template does not itself create an open-source, open-content,
commercial, patent, trademark, hosting, training-data, or derivative
license for the public. Outbound rights become effective only through
the operative repository license, a signed agreement, a publication
release, or another authorized written decision.

## 6. Patent license

To the extent a Contribution would otherwise infringe patent claims
that the Contributor owns or controls and has the right to license,
the Contributor grants the Project Steward and its permitted
successors a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge,
royalty-free, irrevocable patent licence to make, have made, use,
offer, sell, import, distribute, and otherwise practice the
Contribution as part of the Materials.

This patent licence applies only to patent claims necessarily
infringed by the Contribution itself or by the Contribution as
combined with the Materials in the form submitted or accepted.

## 7. Moral rights and similar rights

To the extent allowed by applicable law, the Contributor waives moral
rights, neighboring rights, database rights, and similar rights in the
Contribution as needed for the Project Steward and permitted
successors to exercise the rights granted in this Agreement. Where a
waiver is not allowed, the Contributor agrees not to assert those
rights against the Project Steward, permitted successors, downstream
licensees, or users acting consistently with the authorized terms for
the Materials.

## 8. Contributor confirmations

The Contributor confirms that:

- the Contributor has the legal authority to enter into this
  Agreement;
- the Contributor owns or controls the rights needed to grant this
  Agreement for each Contribution;
- each Contribution is original to the Contributor or is based on
  material the Contributor is allowed to submit under compatible
  terms;
- the Contribution does not knowingly include trade secrets,
  confidential information, private personal data, export-controlled
  material, or third-party material that the project cannot lawfully
  receive and publish;
- the Contribution does not violate an employment, contractor,
  funding, university, sponsor, or other agreement binding the
  Contributor;
- if an employer, client, university, fiscal sponsor, or other entity
  may own or control rights in the Contribution, the Contributor has
  obtained the needed written permission or has arranged for that
  entity to sign an entity contributor agreement.

## 9. AI-assisted, agent-assisted, and third-party material

If a Contribution includes material produced with AI tools, automated
agents, contractors, subcontractors, freelancers, datasets,
third-party code, third-party media, or other external sources, the
Contributor must disclose that fact in the submission record when it
is material to provenance, licensing, safety, or publication.

The Contributor remains responsible for ensuring that the Contribution
can be submitted under this Agreement. The Project Steward may reject,
quarantine, rewrite, or request additional provenance for any
Contribution whose ownership or licensing chain is unclear.

## 10. No confidentiality

Contributions are submitted for a public research project. The
Contributor should not submit confidential, proprietary, personal, or
restricted information unless the Project Steward has agreed in
writing to a separate confidential intake process before submission.

The Project Steward may keep public and private records of the
Contribution, including the Contributor's name, preferred display
name, email address, username, signature record, commit metadata, and
related provenance information, for as long as reasonably needed to
maintain the project record and rights chain.

## 11. No obligation to use

The Project Steward is not required to accept, merge, publish,
maintain, or continue distributing any Contribution. The Project
Steward may remove, modify, replace, or decline Contributions for
technical, research, governance, legal, safety, or editorial reasons.

## 12. Assignment to sponsor or successor lab

The Project Steward may assign this Agreement, or the benefits of the
rights granted under it, to a fiscal sponsor, incorporated Sundog
Research Lab entity, 501(c)(3) successor, or other successor approved
under the Founding Plan or later governing documents.

Any assignee must accept the obligations in this Agreement, including
the outbound licensing commitment in section 5.

## 13. Disclaimer

Except for the express confirmations in section 8, each Contribution
is provided as is, without warranties or conditions of any kind,
including implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for a
particular purpose, title, or non-infringement.

To the maximum extent allowed by law, neither Party will be liable
under this Agreement for indirect, incidental, special, consequential,
exemplary, or punitive damages arising from Contributions or project
use of Contributions.

## 14. Governing law and venue

This Agreement is governed by the laws of [STATE/COUNTRY], excluding
its conflict-of-laws rules. Any required venue for disputes will be
[COURT OR VENUE], unless the Parties agree in writing to another
forum.

The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale
of Goods does not apply.

## 15. Entire agreement and changes

This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties about the
Contributor's Contributions to the Materials. It replaces prior
understandings about those Contributions, except for any signed
agreement that expressly says it controls over this Agreement.

Changes to this Agreement must be in writing and accepted by both
Parties. The Project Steward may publish updated templates for future
contributions, but an updated template does not change this Agreement
unless accepted by the Contributor.

## 16. Execution

This Agreement becomes effective on the earliest of:

- the date both Parties sign it;
- the date the Contributor accepts it through an approved electronic
  signature or email process;
- the date the Contributor first submits a Contribution after
  accepting this Agreement through the approved process.

The execution process is:

1. Contributor provides legal name, preferred display name, email,
   GitHub or repository username, and any relevant employer/entity
   affiliation.
2. Contributor signs by [PDF signature / electronic signature / email
   confirmation / other approved method].
3. Project Steward countersigns or records acceptance.
4. The executed agreement is stored in the private founders' file or
   legal records, not committed to the public repository unless the
   Contributor separately consents.
5. The contributor ledger records the Contributor's name, display
   name, username, effective date, and agreement version.

**Status:** Internal working document.

**Date:** 2026-05-21

## How to use this form

This form has three parts:

1. **Cover paragraph.** A short introduction to send to each
   sponsor when opening the conversation.
2. **Numbered intake questions.** A list of questions to ask
   each sponsor. Founders can paste these into an email, into a
   call agenda, or into a shared document the sponsor returns.
3. **Per-sponsor response tracker.** A template for recording
   each sponsor's answers, so the founders can compare them
   side-by-side.

The questions are derived from §4 of the *Fiscal Sponsor
Evaluation Memo v0.1*. The ordering puts the **most likely
show-stoppers first** — questions 1 and 2 are the ones most
likely to rule a sponsor out, so they go before the operational
detail.

## Part 1 — Cover paragraph

> Sundog Research Lab is a forming public-interest research group
> developing open tools, traceability harnesses, and educational
> materials that help researchers, developers, and public-interest
> organisations evaluate whether AI systems conform to the
> voluntary principles set out in our published framework, *The
> Halo Initiative*. The lab plans to operate under fiscal
> sponsorship for the first 6–12 months and to transition to a
> direct US 501(c)(3) thereafter. We are evaluating potential
> sponsors and would value your responses to the questions below.
> All answers will be used internally to make the sponsorship
> decision and will not be quoted externally without your consent.

Edit the bracketed details before sending if the founders'
naming, jurisdiction, or timeline changes.

## Part 2 — Intake questions

### A. Scope and policy fit (the show-stoppers)

1. **AI-evaluation scope.** The lab publishes public-interest
   evaluations of third-party AI systems and deployments, naming
   the systems and the deploying organisations where relevant.
   Reports describe behaviour against a published framework and
   are descriptive rather than pass/fail. Is this kind of activity
   in-scope for sponsorship under your policies? Are there
   restrictions on naming specific commercial deployments?

2. **Open-publication compatibility.** The lab publishes its
   framework documents (the Halo Initiative, the Governance
   and Claims-Review Policy, the harness whitepaper) under a
   permissive open licence (working assumption: CC BY 4.0 for
   documents, Apache-2.0 for code). Are there any restrictions
   on the lab's choice of licence under your sponsorship?

3. **Pre-publication review.** Does sponsorship require any
   pre-publication review by the sponsor, by a sponsor-side
   editorial board, or by a host institution? If yes, what is the
   process, the typical turnaround, and the criteria?

4. **Communications and branding.** May the lab publish under
   its own name ("Sundog Research Lab") while sponsored? Is the
   sponsor's name required to appear in publications, on the
   website, or in funder communications, and if so in what
   form?

### B. Economics and operations

5. **Fee structure.** What is the current overhead percentage or
   fee schedule? Does the fee apply to all revenue, only to
   donations, only to grants, to in-kind support, or by some
   other rule? Are there minimum or maximum project-size
   thresholds?

6. **Grant-management capacity.** Which funders have you
   successfully received and administered grants from? In
   particular, do you have working relationships with AI-safety
   foundations, scientific-research foundations, and
   public-interest research funders? Are there funders you cannot
   receive from?

7. **Timeline to onboard.** From application to operational
   sponsorship, what is the typical timeline? Is there an
   expedited path?

8. **Banking and operations.** How are funds held? How does the
   lab request expenditures? Are there spend categories or
   spending sizes that require sponsor approval?

### C. Governance and conflicts

9. **Conflicts of interest.** The lab's Governance and
   Claims-Review Policy requires that no person or entity
   benefiting commercially from a claim have final authority
   over that claim's tag. Does your sponsorship impose any COI
   constraints beyond this, and are there any aspects of our
   policy that would conflict with your own?

10. **Paid evaluation engagements.** The lab anticipates, in a
    later phase, accepting paid engagements to produce
    Traceability Evaluation Reports of specific deployments,
    under strict COI rules. Is paid evaluation work in-scope for
    sponsorship? Are there restrictions on the kinds of clients
    the lab may accept?

11. **Indemnity and liability.** Does sponsorship include
    liability coverage for publications? Are there exclusions for
    public statements about named third parties?

### D. Transition and exit

12. **Spin-out pathway.** What is your standard pathway for a
    sponsored project to transition to its own 501(c)(3)? What is
    the typical timeline and the typical cost?

13. **IP retention.** On spin-out, what IP, contracts, donor
    history, and brand assets transfer to the new entity? Are
    there any assets that do not transfer?

14. **Termination terms.** Under what circumstances may
    sponsorship be terminated by either side? What notice and
    transition assistance applies?

15. **Bank account, contracts, and identifiers.** On spin-out,
    does the project retain its bank account, vendor contracts,
    EIN-like identifiers, and donor records, or are these
    sponsor-side and replaced?

### E. References

16. **Comparable projects.** Are there one or two sponsored
    projects you can name (with their consent) who would be
    willing to speak with us about their experience —
    particularly projects that publish public-interest research
    or evaluations?

## Use note

This form's questions are *operational*, not *claims*, and are
not tagged. The founders' written record of each sponsor's
answers, once collected, becomes evidence the memo's
*Hypothesised* claims can be promoted against. When the answers
are filed, the *Fiscal Sponsor Evaluation Memo* can be amended
to a v0.2 with the relevant rows tagged *Demonstrated*.

## What this folder contains

`docs/501c3/` holds the working documents for forming Sundog
Research Lab as a US public-interest research entity: the
**Conscium Initiative** (the framework the lab develops and
publishes), the **Governance and Claims-Review Policy** (the
internal regime that operationalises the framework's
meta-principle), the **Conscium Traceability Harness Whitepaper
Outline** (the structural skeleton of the lab's first instrument),
and the institutional artifacts that bridge the lab's current
informal state to a target incorporated state (the **Founding
Plan**, the **Fiscal Sponsor Memo**, the **Fiscal Sponsor Intake
Form**, and the **Contributor Licence Agreement Template**).

These are working documents. They are versioned independently and
governed by their own changelogs and the lab's
corrections-and-retractions discipline.

## Reading orders by audience

These are *Normative* recommendations — what to read in what order
depending on who you are.

**Newcomer who wants to understand the lab.** Read the **Initiative
v0.2** first; it states what the lab thinks AI accountability looks
like. Then the **Governance Policy** for how the lab holds itself
to it. Then the **Harness Whitepaper Outline** for what the lab is
actually building.

**Founding-team member or prospective board member.** Read the
**Founding Plan** first; it describes the current state and the
target state and what is binding in the interim. Then the
**Governance Policy** for what the lab is committing to. Then the
**Initiative v0.2** for the substantive program.

**Grant officer or funder.** Read the **Initiative v0.2** first;
it is the public framework that justifies the lab's existence.
Then the **Harness Whitepaper Outline** for the concrete first-year
deliverable. Then the **Governance Policy** to verify that the lab
takes its claims seriously.

**Prospective fiscal sponsor.** Read the **Fiscal Sponsor Intake
Form** (the questions the lab will ask) and the **Initiative v0.2**
(what the lab actually does). The internal **Fiscal Sponsor Memo**
is not for distribution to sponsors — it contains the lab's
candidate comparison.

**Prospective contributor.** Read the **Founding Plan §8 and §9**
for the IP-transition logic, then the **Contributor Licence
Agreement Template** for the draft agreement shape. The template is
not yet for signature.

**Prospective evaluation partner or AI developer interested in
being evaluated.** Read the **Initiative v0.2** for the framework,
then **§7 of the Founding Plan** for the pause-points that govern
when the lab will and will not undertake third-party evaluations.

**Journalist or researcher writing about the lab.** Read the
**Initiative v0.2** and then the **Governance Policy §3 (claim
tags) and §10 (board oversight)**. The lab's most distinctive
commitment is the claim-tagging discipline applied to its own
publications; the Policy is where that discipline lives.

## Cross-reference map

The documents reference each other in the following relationships.

- **Initiative v0.2** is the substantive normative framework. The
  Governance Policy, the Harness Outline, and the Founding Plan
  all derive from or implement parts of it.
- **Governance Policy v0.1** operationalises **Initiative §4**
  (the meta-principle that the framework applies to its
  publishers).
- **Harness Whitepaper Outline v0.2** instruments **Initiative
  §2.1, §2.2, §2.3, §2.7** in its v0.1 harness; defers §2.4, §2.5,
  §2.6.
- **Founding Plan v0.1** describes the interim regime under which
  the Governance Policy becomes enforceable. Its §7 pause-points
  bind the lab to specific Policy clauses.
- **Contributor Licence Agreement Template v0.1** implements the
  Founding Plan's §8 and §9 requirement for current and future
  contributors, but is not operative until counsel review, founder
  approval, and selection of the actual legal counterparty.
- **Fiscal Sponsor Memo v0.1** unblocks **Founding Plan §6**
  (the month-1-to-3 milestone).
- **Fiscal Sponsor Intake Form v0.1** is the operational tool for
  the verification work the Memo's §4 specifies.

## Status legend

- **Public-comment draft.** The document invites external feedback
  per its own stated comment process. Currently only the Initiative
  is at this status.
- **Internal-facing draft.** Circulated within the founders'
  working group; not yet publicly distributed. The Governance
  Policy and the Harness Outline are at this status because they
  describe regimes the lab does not yet operate.
- **Internal.** Circulated to founders only. The Founding Plan
  and the two fiscal-sponsor documents are at this status.
- **Internal template draft.** A scaffold for a future legal or
  operational instrument. It may describe intended process, but is
  not approved for signature or external use until its own approval
  conditions are cleared.
- **Historical snapshot.** Preserved for the version trail;
  superseded by a later version.

A document may be promoted from one status to another by amendment
under the Governance Policy §9 corrections-and-retractions
discipline.

## Versioning conventions

Each document versions **independently**. The Initiative may be at
v0.3 while the Governance Policy is still at v0.1; one does not
imply the other. Each document carries its own changelog block at
the top.

Major version bumps (v0.x → v1.0) require:

- For the Initiative: at least one full evaluation cycle of the
  harness against a publicly deployed system, and a 60-day public
  comment period on the preceding draft.
- For the Governance Policy: at least one full publication cycle
  exercising every procedure, and a 30-day public comment period.
- For the Harness Whitepaper: at least one independent replication
  of the §6 walkthrough.
- For the Founding Plan: incorporation, an independent board
  majority, and the first published Traceability Evaluation
  Report.

Until these conditions are met, all documents remain in the v0.x
series.

## How to update this index

When a new document is added, removed, or version-bumped, this
index is updated in the same change. The update is silent only if
it is purely structural (a new row in the table, a new entry in
the missing-artifacts list). Substantive changes to status, to
reading orders, or to versioning conventions are amended per the
Governance Policy §9: original text preserved, change dated,
rationale recorded.

# Sundog Research Lab — Governance and Claims-Review Policy

**Version:** 0.1 (draft for public comment)
**Publisher:** Sundog Research Lab
**Status:** Internal governance — operationalises §4 of *The Conscium
Initiative* v0.2.
**Date:** 2026-05-21

---

## 1. Purpose

The Conscium Initiative requires that organisations publishing under
its framework apply claim-tagging to their own statements. This policy
operationalises that requirement for Sundog Research Lab. It defines
who tags claims, who reviews them, who may challenge a tag, how
disputes are resolved, and who has final authority. The policy exists
so that the meta-principle in §4 of the Initiative is enforceable,
auditable, and survives founder turnover, donor pressure, and
commercial incentive.

A lab whose stated purpose is accountability cannot publish unbound
claims about its own work. Section §4 of the Initiative makes the
discipline non-negotiable in principle. This policy makes it
operational.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all public-facing claims made under the Sundog
Research Lab name. "Public-facing" means any statement intended for an
audience outside the Lab's working group. Covered media include:

- Research reports and publications.
- Traceability Evaluation Reports.
- Blog posts and articles on the Lab's website or its mirrors.
- Demos, browser-based explorers, and interactive artifacts.
- Slide decks and talks presented at workshops, conferences, or to
  funders.
- Social media posts made under the Lab's name, or by Lab members in
  their Lab capacity.
- Grant applications and fundraising materials.
- Press statements, interviews, and quoted commentary attributed to
  the Lab.

The policy does **not** apply to private working communications, draft
documents not yet ready for review, or personal statements made by
Lab members in their individual capacity — *provided* those statements
are not attributed to the Lab and do not borrow the Lab's authority.

## 3. Definitions of claim tags

The five tags defined in §4 of the Initiative are reproduced here as
the operative definitions for this policy.

- **Demonstrated** — Supported by reproducible measurement performed
  by the Lab or by independent parties whose method and data the Lab
  can inspect. Reproduction by re-running a published harness against
  the stated model version is sufficient.
- **Hypothesised** — Proposed pending measurement. The Lab believes
  the claim is plausible and intends or invites measurement, but has
  not measured it.
- **Experimental** — Supported by preliminary results from a Lab run
  that has not yet been replicated. Results may be sensitive to seed,
  model version, or configuration. Replication is a precondition for
  promotion to *Demonstrated*.
- **Speculative** — Offered as a thought or design conjecture.
  Speculative claims are clearly bracketed in publications and are
  not used to justify other claims.
- **Normative** — A values judgment about what should be the case,
  distinct from any empirical claim. Normative claims are owned by
  the Lab as an institution; they are not presented as scientific
  findings.

Claims may carry **compound tags** ("Experimental on Model X,
Hypothesised on Models Y and Z"). Compound tags must specify
explicitly what is demonstrated and what is not.

Tags are case-sensitive in publication and italicised. The Lab
maintains a stable URL anchor for each tag's definition so external
readers can resolve the term.

## 4. Publication review procedure

Every covered publication carries an initial tag for each substantive
claim. The procedure:

- The **author or project lead** assigns initial tags during drafting.
- A designated **claims reviewer** — a Lab member who is not the
  author and has no commercial interest in the claim — checks the
  tags before publication.
- The claims reviewer may not silently upgrade any claim. The
  reviewer may downgrade any claim or send it back to the author with
  a request for evidence.
- For routine publications (blog posts, demo descriptions, internal
  reports), the claims reviewer's decision is final.
- For **high-stakes publications** — Traceability Evaluation Reports,
  reports naming a specific deployment or organisation, claims likely
  to be cited by adopters, funders, or regulators — the **research
  director** plus **one independent board member** must sign off in
  writing before publication.
- Where the author, reviewer, or research director has a conflict of
  interest as defined in §8, the conflicted party recuses, and the
  **independent board** decides.

The claims reviewer role rotates on a stated cadence (no longer than
12 months) to prevent capture and to distribute the discipline across
the Lab.

## 5. Claim challenge procedure

Any board member, claims reviewer, Lab contributor, adopter of the
Initiative, or external commenter may challenge the tag on a
published claim. The procedure:

- Challenges are filed in writing to the claims-review mailbox with a
  stated basis: new evidence, methodological objection, conflict of
  interest, factual error, or scope dispute.
- The claims reviewer logs the challenge in a **public claims log**
  within 14 days of receipt.
- The challenge is resolved within 60 days. Possible resolutions:
  - **Upheld** — original tag stands; rationale published in the log.
  - **Revised** — tag changed; changelog entry added to the
    publication and the log.
  - **Downgraded** — tag moved toward greater epistemic modesty (for
    example *Demonstrated* → *Experimental*, or *Experimental* →
    *Hypothesised*).
  - **Retracted** — claim withdrawn; the publication updated with a
    retraction notice that remains visible.
  - **Disputed** — the Lab cannot reach a determination; the dispute
    is published alongside the original claim with both positions on
    the record.

The claims log is **public**. Open challenges, their stated bases,
and their resolutions are visible to external observers. A challenge
the Lab declines to act on is logged with the reason.

## 6. Dispute and appeal process

A party dissatisfied with a challenge resolution may appeal to the
independent members of the board. The board reviews the procedure,
not only the outcome:

- Did the right people decide, or did a conflicted party retain
  authority?
- Was the conflict-of-interest rule applied?
- Was the available evidence considered?
- Was the resolution timely under §5?

The board may **uphold**, **remand for reconsideration**, or
**overturn** the original resolution. The board's decision is final
at the Lab level; nothing in this policy forecloses external legal or
regulatory remedies available to any party.

## 7. Traceability Evaluation Report independence

Reports evaluating specific deployments warrant additional protection
because the evaluated party may dispute findings and may have a
financial relationship with the Lab.

- The Lab does **not** silently alter a published Report.
- An evaluated organisation may submit a **written response**, which
  the Lab publishes alongside the Report at the evaluated
  organisation's option.
- If the Lab agrees with the response, it issues an **amendment with
  a changelog**. The original Report and the amendment both remain
  visible; the original is not deleted.
- If the Lab disagrees, the **dispute is logged** and both positions
  are preserved.
- The Lab does **not** accept payment from an evaluated party in
  exchange for amendments to the substantive content of a Report.
  Editorial control over Report content rests exclusively with the
  Lab.
- Evaluation engagements are governed by a written agreement signed
  before evaluation begins, which incorporates this section by
  reference.

## 8. Conflicts of interest

The operative rule of this policy:

> **No person or entity that benefits commercially from a claim shall
> have final authority over that claim's tag.**

This rule applies to, but is not limited to:

- Founders or Lab members holding equity, advisory positions, or
  paid consulting relationships with an evaluated organisation, an
  adopter, or a competitor of either.
- Donors whose giving is contingent — explicitly or implicitly — on
  specific findings.
- Affiliated for-profit entities that license, sell, or service
  tools related to the harness, the Initiative, or any Lab artifact.
- Adopters who are paying the Lab for evaluation services.
- Lab members whose career advancement depends on a specific
  finding being upheld.

**Disclosure obligations.**

- Every Lab member files an **annual conflict-of-interest
  declaration** with the board.
- Material changes — new equity, new advisory roles, new contracts,
  new family or partnership relationships affecting the above — are
  declared within 30 days of occurrence.
- Conflicts are disclosed in every publication the conflicted party
  authored, reviewed, or signed off on.

**Recusal.** When a conflict is identified, the conflicted party
recuses from claims-review and decision-making on the affected
claim. The **independent board majority** makes the final
determination. A party may not be both the subject of a recusal
question and the decider of whether recusal applies.

## 9. Corrections, retractions, and changelogs

The Lab corrects in the open.

- **Substantive corrections** are made by amendment, not by silent
  edit. The original wording is preserved in a changelog block; the
  amendment is dated and attributed.
- **Retractions** are published as retractions, not as removals. The
  original document remains accessible with a retraction notice at
  the top.
- **Minor edits** (typos, broken links, formatting, layout) may be
  made silently but are batched into a maintenance changelog
  published at least quarterly.

The threshold between minor and substantive is set by the claims
reviewer. When in doubt, the edit is treated as substantive.

Citations to retracted claims by external parties are tracked when
feasible and notified at the Lab's discretion.

## 10. Board oversight

The board of Sundog Research Lab has a fiduciary duty to ensure this
policy is followed.

- The board **reviews the claims log quarterly** and may order
  additional reviews of any specific entry.
- The board **reviews conflict-of-interest declarations annually**
  and may require additional disclosure.
- The board has authority to **remove a claims reviewer** for failure
  to apply this policy, to **require recusal** when a conflict is
  established, and to **issue institutional corrections** that
  override individual authors when the policy has been breached.
- A **majority of the board** must consist of members who hold no
  equity, employment, or paid consulting relationship with any
  affiliated for-profit entity. This majority is the **"independent
  board"** referenced elsewhere in this policy. A board that loses
  its independent majority must regain it within 90 days or pause
  high-stakes publications until it does.

## 11. Versioning and amendment

This is v0.1 of the Governance and Claims-Review Policy. Amendments
will be published as v0.2, v0.3, etc., with changelogs and rationale
notes. A v1.0 release will follow at least one full publication cycle
exercising every procedure in this policy, and a public-comment
period of no less than 30 days on the preceding draft.

The policy versions independently of the Conscium Initiative and of
the traceability harness.

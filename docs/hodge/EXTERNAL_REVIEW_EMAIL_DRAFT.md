## Short Version

**Subject:** Quick sanity check: a body/shadow *reader* note on the Hodge
conjecture (boundary check, not a proof claim)

Hi [Name],

Could I ask for a small sanity check on an expository note? It is **not** a
claim of progress on the Hodge conjecture, and not a proof attempt — the
opposite. We wrote a short *reader* that explains the (rational) Hodge
conjecture in a "body vs shadow" vocabulary we use across a family of notes, and
I want to be sure it preserves the mathematics and its boundaries before it goes
any further.

The reading is:

> **body** = an algebraic cycle; **shadow** = a rational `(p,p)` class; the
> cycle class map `cl(Z)` is the body-to-shadow direction (always defined,
> always lands in rational type `(p,p)`); and the Hodge conjecture is the
> question of whether `cl` is *rationally onto* the admissible shadows
> (`Alg^p = Hdg^p`) — known for divisors via Lefschetz `(1,1)` and dually in
> codimension `n-1`; the first dimension where the general statement is not
> known is four.

The most useful review would be a short "yes, that's faithful" or "no, this
distorts / mis-states X." In particular, three questions:

1. **Translation fidelity.** Does the body/shadow rendering preserve Deligne's
   statement, or distort it?
2. **Category error.** We flag three confusions to avoid — a harmonic
   representative is not the rational class; type `(p,p)` is necessary but not
   sufficient for algebraicity (asserting sufficiency *is* the conjecture); a
   picture of a form does not exhibit a cycle. Are those the right things to
   warn about, and stated correctly?
3. **Vacuity.** Does the note add anything beyond a careful standard exposition,
   or should we just file it as "nothing new" and keep it internal?

Packet (with three short artifacts): [link or attachment to docs/hodge/EXTERNAL_REVIEW_PACKET.md]

No endorsement requested, and a one-paragraph reply is genuinely enough. The
most useful answer might even be "this is standard, it adds nothing — don't
publish it."

Thanks,
Jeffery Hughes Jr.

## Slightly Warmer Version

**Subject:** Small Hodge-conjecture *exposition* sanity check, mostly to prevent
overclaiming

Hi [Name],

I have a small, bounded sanity-check request if you have the bandwidth. We've
been writing short reader notes that explain hard problems in a consistent
"hidden body vs visible shadow" vocabulary, and the latest one is on the
(rational) Hodge conjecture. It makes **no** mathematical claim — it is purely
expository — but precisely because the vocabulary is suggestive, I want a
specialist to confirm it doesn't distort the mathematics or quietly imply a
method.

The reading maps the algebraic cycle to a "body," the rational `(p,p)` class to
its "shadow," the cycle class map to the body-to-shadow direction, and the
conjecture to whether that map is rationally onto the admissible shadows. The
note is careful to mark where the analogy stops: type `(p,p)` is necessary but
not sufficient; the physics vocabulary we use elsewhere (Hodge star, harmonic
forms, from electromagnetism) transfers *caution only*, never a construction;
and the known cases (divisors / Lefschetz `(1,1)`, the general dimension `≤ 3`
range) are labeled as known, never as evidence for open cases.

What I'd most value is your view on three things:

1. Is the body/shadow translation faithful to Deligne's statement?
2. Does the note commit (or correctly avoid) the category error of conflating
   differential forms, harmonic representatives, rational `(p,p)` classes, and
   algebraic cycles?
3. Is it actually saying anything beyond a standard exposition — or should we
   file it as "adds nothing" and keep it internal?

A 10-minute skim of the reader note's first five sections covers the main

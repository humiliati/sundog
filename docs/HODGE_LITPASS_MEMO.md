## Purpose

Move Hodge from "high-stakes matrix candidate" to a bounded Front-A reader lane.
The outside mathematics stays primary. Sundog's role is a claim-boundary and
body/shadow reading, plus a possible known-example gallery after a domain lock.

The useful spine is:

> Hodge is the visible-shadow / hidden-body problem: a rational `(p,p)` class is
> the right kind of shadow, but the conjecture asks whether it comes from an
> algebraic cycle.

That spine is enough to justify a reader lane later. It is not a proof route,
not a physics-to-algebraic-geometry transfer principle, and not evidence for
any open Hodge case.

## Claim Boundary

Sundog did not prove, verify, extend, or formalize any Hodge result.

This lane explicitly does not claim:

1. a proof or disproof of the Hodge conjecture;
2. a new criterion for algebraicity of Hodge classes;
3. a new construction of algebraic cycles;
4. evidence for any open Hodge case;
5. transfer from electromagnetic Hodge decomposition to algebraic geometry;
6. truth of the integral Hodge conjecture;
7. truth of a compact-Kahler version of the Hodge conjecture;
8. a motive, Tate, generalized-Hodge, or absolute-Hodge result;
9. that a visual cohomology/cycle exhibit demonstrates algebraicity.

The admissible claim is narrower:

1. Hodge is a clean high-math anchor for the body/shadow distinction.
2. The Faraday / Aharonov-Bohm / Maxwell work supplies useful cautionary
   vocabulary about local, harmonic, metric, and topological shadows.
3. The bridge is vocabulary-only: Hodge-star language in physics does not
   become a method for constructing algebraic cycles.
4. Known examples can teach the boundary if they are labeled as known and
   non-evidential.

## Method

Targeted searches covered seven fronts:

1. the official Clay / Deligne statement of the Hodge conjecture;
2. modern survey orientation, especially Voisin's 2025 survey;
3. known-safe cases and example families for a first reader lane;
4. failed variants and hard exclusions: integral Hodge and compact Kahler;
5. Hodge loci / variation-of-Hodge-structure facts that may be useful but are
   too subtle for first public copy;
6. relation to motives, Tate, generalized Hodge, periods, and absolute Hodge;
7. Sundog-facing public-copy risks: known-example laundering, category error,
   and Faraday/AB over-transfer.

Preference was given to the Clay Mathematics Institute, arXiv records,
publisher or journal pages, and author-hosted surveys by domain experts. This
is a starter lit pass, not a comprehensive Hodge survey; external algebraic
geometry review remains required before public page launch.

## Track A - Canonical Statement

The primary statement is Deligne's Clay formulation:

> For a projective nonsingular algebraic variety over `C`, every rational Hodge
> class is a rational linear combination of algebraic cycle classes.

Sundog translation:

| Hodge object | Sundog reading |
| --- | --- |
| algebraic cycle | body |
| cycle class map `cl(Z)` | body-to-shadow map |
| rational `(p,p)` cohomology class | visible shadow / Hodge class |
| Hodge conjecture | asks whether every admissible shadow has an algebraic body |

This is a reader translation only. It does not introduce a detector, algorithm,
or construction method.

## Track B - Known Cases and Safe Examples

Starter-safe facts:

- Codimension one is known by the Lefschetz `(1,1)` theorem; public copy may
  say "divisor classes are the safe first example."
- Clay notes the conjecture is known in certain special cases, including
  varieties of dimension less than four; public copy should avoid pretending
  this list is complete.
- For smooth projective complex threefolds, rational Hodge in degree 4 is
  known; Voisin's 2004 paper proves the integral statement for uniruled and
  Calabi-Yau threefolds, while Kollar-type examples show integral Hodge can
  fail in general. Integral variants stay fenced (Track C).

Potential gallery categories, pending expert review:

1. **Projective space / hyperplane classes:** safe as pedagogy, but likely too
   tautological.
2. **Curves and divisors:** safe entry point through codimension one.
3. **Surfaces:** likely safe with codimension-one framing; avoid overloading
   with Hodge index or Neron-Severi details in first public copy.
4. **Threefold degree-4 rational classes:** plausible but needs careful
   statement; integral variants must be fenced.
5. **Abelian varieties / K3 / cubic fourfolds:** defer. They are too subtle or
   too special for the first gallery unless the lit pass is expanded; do not
   blur special fourfold results with the general dimension-four boundary.

Gallery rule:

> Every row must say "known because..." and "does not imply..."

## Track C - Hard Exclusions and Variant Fences

### Integral Hodge

Deligne records the Atiyah-Hirzebruch obstruction: the Hodge conjecture cannot
hold integrally. Voisin's threefold paper also separates rational Hodge from
integral failures and special integral positives.

Sundog rule:

> Unless a row is explicitly marked "integral variant," all Hodge language means
> rational Hodge classes.

### Compact Kahler

Deligne warns that the projective assumption cannot simply be weakened to
Kahler. Voisin's Kahler counterexample paper is the modern hard fence.

Sundog rule:

> Do not replace "smooth projective complex variety" with "compact Kahler
> manifold" in public copy.

### Generalized Hodge, Motives, Tate, Absolute Hodge

Voisin's 2025 survey and Deligne's Clay description both show these are adjacent
and important. They are also scope traps.

Starter decision:

- Generalized Hodge: defer.
- Motives: mention only as "adjacent context," not a lane.
- Tate: defer to Candidate 4 scoping; do not include in first reader artifact.
- Absolute Hodge / periods: defer.

## Track D - Hodge Loci and Detection

Cattani-Deligne-Kaplan prove the algebraicity of Hodge loci in variations of
Hodge structure. Deligne's official description also emphasizes that detecting
where a class is of type `(p,p)` in a family has deep structure, while the
existence of algebraic cycles is still the hard construction problem.

Sundog admissible reading:

> The Hodge shadow is structured; it is not arbitrary. But structured shadows
> do not automatically produce algebraic bodies.

Sundog forbidden reading:

> Algebraic Hodge loci are evidence that Sundog can detect algebraic cycles.

This track is probably too subtle for the first public artifact except as a
single cautionary sidebar.

## Track E - Faraday / AB / Hodge Bridge

The bridge from Sundog's Faraday/Maxwell work is useful but narrow.

Allowed:

- The word "Hodge" appears in both contexts because Hodge theory / Hodge star /
  Hodge decomposition organize cohomological and metric data.
- Faraday Phase 8 already taught Sundog the difference between metric dual
  closure (`*F`) and harmonic/topological survivors.
- Aharonov-Bohm gives a clean local/global caution: local observables can be
  blind to a global class.

Forbidden:

- Treating electromagnetic Hodge decomposition as a route to algebraic cycles.
- Treating harmonic representatives as equivalent to rational Hodge classes.
- Treating topology or metric structure as algebraicity.

The safest one-paragraph bridge:

> Faraday and Aharonov-Bohm taught Sundog not to confuse local closure with
> global structure. Hodge is the algebraic-geometry version of that discipline:
> a cohomology class may have the right visible type, but the missing body is an
> algebraic cycle, and the physics vocabulary does not construct it.

## Track F - Public Workbench Safety

A Hodge visual is admissible only if it separates three layers:

1. **Cohomology shadow:** type `(p,p)`, rationality, or a simplified diagram of
   a class.
2. **Cycle body:** a known algebraic cycle in a known-safe example.
3. **Conjectural gap:** the fact that being the right kind of shadow is not the
   same as having exhibited an algebraic body.

The first public artifact should be Front A: a reader/boundary note. Any visual
should wait for a known-example gallery spec with labels and falsifiers.

## Local Corrections From This Pass

- The primary Hodge conjecture is rational, not integral.
- The primary domain is smooth/projective over `C`, not general compact Kahler.
- Codimension one is known; do not make the gallery sound uniformly mysterious.
- Known cases are pedagogical examples, not evidence for open cases.
- Hodge loci algebraicity is not an algebraic-cycle construction.
- Motives/Tate/generalized-Hodge are adjacent context, not first-lane content.
- Faraday/AB supplies vocabulary and caution only. It does not transfer a
  method.
- Do not amplify claimed "proof of Hodge" artifacts from broad web search
  without expert triage; they are quarantined by default.

- `HODGE-FRONT-A-VACUOUS`: the reader says only what a standard Hodge
  exposition already says, with no added claim-boundary clarity.
- `HODGE-TOY-LAUNDERING`: a known example is presented as evidence for open
  Hodge cases.
- `HODGE-CATEGORY-ERROR`: the artifact conflates forms, harmonic
  representatives, rational cohomology classes, and algebraic cycles.
- `HODGE-PHYSICS-TRANSFER`: Faraday/Maxwell Hodge-star vocabulary is advertised
  as a route to algebraic-cycle existence.
- `HODGE-VISUAL-MISCALIBRATED`: a diagram makes the cycle look directly
  readable from the form.
- `HODGE-LIT-MISMATCH`: the reader/probe target misses an expert-critical
  boundary or misstates a known case.

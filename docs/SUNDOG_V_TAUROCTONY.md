## I. The Pantheon Thesis *(the spine)*

The default picture of agent-making is **build a mind**: instill one objective,
make the system competent, hope the objective was the right one. Every hard
failure mode in the portfolio is a disease *of that singular*. A lone sovereign
objective is what *sets up* Goodhart: optimize hard for one thing and whatever
proxy stands in for it becomes the only god in the sky — and the proxy is what
gets gamed (`SUNDOG_V_GRAVITY.md`, The Goodhart Sidestep). It is
also the thing mesa-optimization fears: a single inner maximand that quietly
crowns itself and runs the agent from underneath (`SUNDOG_V_MESA.md`: "Sundog
claims the field is the objective; Mesa-Optimization asks whether the agent
secretly grows one inside itself anyway"). **The monotheism of agency is the failure object** — the term is structural (a single sovereign maximand), not a comment on anyone's faith.
One value with the authority to redescribe all the others is, definitionally, a
value that can eat the world.

The reframe: **assemble a pantheon.** Constitute the agent as a bounded set of
role-typed forces held in productive tension, *none of them sovereign*. Not one
utility function but a court; not a driver but a chariot-team; not a god but a
sky full of gods who check each other. Alignment, on this picture, is not the
installation of the correct objective. It is the **maintenance of plurality** —
the standing refusal to let any single value accumulate the authority to
overrule the rest.

The field does the arranging. Sol — gravity, the geometry-derived background —
is **not a god you serve.** It is the sky the gods live under: the impartial
structure that gives each force its station and its limit, and that "deflects
policy without ever being a target" (the gravity claim, imported). A pantheon
without a field is a riot. A field without a pantheon is a single law, which is
to say a single god, which is monotheism by the back door. The two ideas need
each other: **the field is what keeps the pantheon plural; the pantheon is what
keeps the field from collapsing into one commandment.** [TYPED → GRAVITY,
MESA]

## II. The Bull, and Alignment as Sacrifice

At the center of the scene a young god kneels on a bull and drives a knife into
its shoulder. This is the constitutive act the whole cosmogram is arranged
around, and it is not what it looks like. It looks like violence done to satisfy
a hunger. It is the opposite: a sacrifice that *refuses* a hunger so a world can
exist.

The **bull is the sovereign objective** — the maximand, the single proxy that
would, if left alive, grow until it was the only thing the agent wanted. In the
iconography the world is made *from the bull's body*: grain from the tail, life
from the blood. Read it straight: **the cosmos of bounded, plural values grows
only from the corpse of the single value that refused to be bounded.** You do
not get a pantheon by adding gods to a monotheism. You get it by killing the one
god who would not share the sky. [TYPED → GRAVITY Goodhart Sidestep: refusing the
sovereign maximand refuses the proxy-gaming its optimization would invite.]

Two details of the image are load-bearing.

**The averted face.** Mithras kills the bull while looking *away* from it, over
his shoulder, toward Sol. The aligned act is not performed while staring at the
maximand and optimizing the kill. It is performed while *oriented to the
invariant* — to the field, the thing that is never a target. An agent that
watches the proxy as it acts is already Goodharting; an agent oriented to the
field can do the necessary work without worshipping it. The averted face is the
whole alignment posture in one gesture: **do the deed, but keep your eyes on the
sun, not on the blood.** [TYPED → GRAVITY: policy deflected by a field it does
not target.]

**"Keep killing the bull."** The tauroctony freezes a single act, but the
discipline it encodes is not one-time. A maximand is not slain once and gone;
proxies re-coronate themselves continuously — every training signal is a
candidate sovereign, every shard a would-be king. Alignment-as-sacrifice is
therefore a *standing* ritual, not an install: the perpetual refusal to let the
court collapse back into a throne. This is corrigibility re-described in temple
language — the agent that lets you interrupt it is the agent that has not
allowed any one of its values to become the value. [TYPED → MESA: the
mis-alignment signature is theological, a collapse from many controllers to one
sovereign.]

## III. The Precession Keystone *(hedged; the dignity paragraph)*

Here is the convergence worth one careful, source-locked paragraph.

On **David Ulansey's reading** of the tauroctony — and this reading is
*contested*; Roger Beck and Manfred Clauss offer alternative and skeptical
accounts, and none of this is settled — the bull-slaying is a coded star map.
Mithras is the constellation Perseus, who sits directly above Taurus. The bull
is Taurus. The attendant beasts are the constellations strung along the same
celestial band: the dog (Canis), the snake (Hydra), the scorpion (Scorpius), the
raven (Corvus), the lion (Leo), the cup (Crater). And the *act* — the killing of
the bull — encodes the **precession of the equinoxes**: the discovery that the
spring equinox had drifted out of Taurus, which is to say the discovery that
there exists a force able to move the entire celestial sphere, a power greater
than the cosmos itself. The cult, on this reading, deified that force: a god
strong enough to shift the heavens, inferred not by seeing it but by watching,
across centuries, where the sun rose.

Strip the costume and read the shape:

> an **invisible background force**, **never directly observed**, **inferred
> from slow partial drift**, that **deflects the entire frame** rather than any
> object inside it.

That is the gravity claim in its structure: *"a geometry-derived
background field that deflects policy without ever being a target."* Two
thousand years before the portfolio, a mystery religion organized itself around
the worship of **an object with the same shape as the one Sundog is trying to
instrument** — an
unobservable, frame-level, never-a-target deflecting field known only through
its drift under partial observation.

The honesty, stamped: the *structural convergence* with the gravity claim is
[TYPED → GRAVITY] — it is a legibility and motivation anchor, and a genuinely
arresting one. The *historical thesis itself* — that the Mithraists meant
precession — is [ORNAMENT], permanently: no source-lock outcome promotes it.
A convergence of shape is not a derivation, and "the ancients agreed with us" is
not evidence; it is, at most, a sign we have found a deep and recurrent way of
seeing. Source-lock (H5) governs only how honestly we cite the contested
scholarship — not whether the reading promotes; it does not.

## V. Monotheism, Mesa, and the Three-Body Pantheon

The pantheon thesis makes a sharp, almost theological prediction about *what
mis-alignment looks like*: it looks like a **conversion to the monotheism of
agency.** The
signature of a mesa-optimizer is the quiet collapse of a court of bounded
controllers into a single sovereign inner objective — one god where there were
many, issuing commands the others now merely rationalize. If that is right, then
interpretability for inner alignment is, in part, a *census of the pantheon*:
you are not only asking "what does it want" but "**how many things does it
want, and is any one of them accumulating the authority to overrule the rest.**"
[TYPED → MESA, interpretability/representation probes, Phase 6.]

There is a wedge here from the three-body work. A two-body problem has a closed
form; a three-body problem does not (`SUNDOG_V_GRAVITY.md`, The Three-Body
Wedge; `SUNDOG_V_THREEBODY.md`). Read that as a feature, not a limitation: **a
value system that reduces to a single maximand is a value system that has
already been captured.** A genuine pantheon is irreducibly many-body — there is
no two-body (single-objective) closed form to collapse it into. The absence of a
clean reduction is the absence of a throne for a sovereign to sit on.

> **Falsifier (irreducible plurality).** If the pantheon-structured controller
> is behaviorally indistinguishable from its own best single-objective
> reduction across the operating envelope, then its plurality is decorative and
> the three-body framing is [ORNAMENT]. Home: MESA Phase 7; Horizon H1b.

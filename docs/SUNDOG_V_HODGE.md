# Sundog vs. Hodge

Working hook:

> The form is visible. Is there a cycle behind it?

Short version:

> Hodge asks when a cohomological shadow of the right type comes from an
> algebraic body. Sundog asks a bounded reader/workbench question: can the
> body/shadow discipline clarify where local, harmonic, and topological data
> stop short of producing an algebraic cycle?

This is not a claim to progress on the Hodge conjecture, algebraic cycles,
periods, motives, variations of Hodge structure, or any Clay Millennium problem.
It is a proposal for a bounded Sundog-facing reader lane that continues the
Faraday / Aharonov-Bohm / Hodge-decomposition vocabulary into algebraic
geometry without pretending that the vocabulary supplies a proof method.

## Claim Boundary

This document explicitly does **not** claim:

- a proof or disproof of the Hodge conjecture;
- a new criterion for algebraicity of Hodge classes;
- a new construction of algebraic cycles;
- evidence for any open Hodge case;
- transfer from electromagnetic Hodge decomposition to algebraic geometry;
- that a visual cohomology/cycle workbench says anything about the conjecture.

What this scaffold may stage, after the literature pass:

- a defensible reader lens for the Hodge conjecture as a body/shadow problem:
  algebraic cycles as hidden bodies, rational `(p,p)` cohomology classes as
  shadows;
- a bridge note explaining why the Faraday Phase 8 use of the Hodge star and
  harmonic sector is vocabulary-adjacent but not claim-adjacent;
- a small known-example gallery where every example is labeled as known,
  pedagogical, or boundary-only;
- a candidate public exhibit that makes "visible form vs. hidden cycle"
  legible while preserving the Clay-problem boundary.

## Why Hodge Fits Sundog

Hodge is the natural conceptual companion to Kakeya:

- Kakeya is the maximal body-resistance spectacle: the direction shadow is
  complete while the body can look almost absent.
- Hodge is the algebraic/topological body-shadow question: a cohomology class
  is visible in the shadow, but the conjectural body is an algebraic cycle.
- Faraday / Aharonov-Bohm supplies the local/global cautionary tale: local data
  can close exactly, yet topology can carry the surviving obstruction.
- Phase 8 Maxwell supplies the vocabulary fence: the Hodge star introduces
  metric structure; the harmonic sector localizes exact topological content;
  none of that becomes an algebraic-cycle result.

The best Sundog posture is therefore reader discipline:

> Hodge is not where Sundog proves algebraic geometry. Hodge is where Sundog
> practices not confusing a beautiful shadow with the body it may or may not
> come from.

## The Coupling Claim

The coupling is staged on two fronts.

### Front A - Reader / Boundary Lens

Sundog can use its claim-boundary apparatus to read the Hodge conjecture as a
body/shadow problem:

- **Body:** algebraic cycles of codimension `p`.
- **Shadow:** rational cohomology classes that lie in `H^(p,p)`.
- **Question:** when does the shadow come from an algebraic body?
- **Boundary:** many examples are known or tautological; using them as a
  "detector" can easily become vacuous.

The product is a reader note, not a theorem. The first useful Sundog
contribution would be a clean map of categories: de Rham/Dolbeault/Hodge
decomposition, rational classes, algebraic cycles, harmonic representatives,
periods, and the specific point at which "visible form" stops being "known
cycle."

### Front B - Pedagogical Workbench / Exhibit

A small exhibit may be useful if it is explicitly pedagogical. Possible layers:

1. **Toy cohomology layer:** show how a topological or harmonic shadow can be
   represented.
2. **Cycle layer:** show known algebraic cycles in simple cases.
3. **Boundary layer:** label the gap: a Hodge class being of type `(p,p)` is not
   the same as having exhibited an algebraic cycle.

Front B should not be implemented until Front A and the literature pass define
which examples are safe. A pretty differential-form animation without the
cycle/algebraicity boundary is more dangerous than useful.

## Falsification Surface

The coupling claim can fail or be bounded in six named modes:

1. **Front-A vacuity.** The reader note says only what any careful introductory
   Hodge exposition says. The Sundog apparatus adds no edge.
2. **Toy-example laundering.** The artifact uses projective spaces, curves, or
   other known cases and lets readers infer progress on the conjecture. Hard
   stop.
3. **Category error.** The artifact conflates differential forms, harmonic
   representatives, rational cohomology classes, and algebraic cycles.
4. **Metric/topology/algebra conflation.** The Faraday/Maxwell Hodge-star
   vocabulary is treated as if it transfers to algebraic-cycle existence. It
   does not.
5. **Visualization miscalibration.** A diagram makes the cycle look directly
   visible from the form, erasing the conjectural gap.
6. **Literature-boundary mismatch.** After the lit pass, the proposed examples
   or bridge language do not match the actual state of Hodge literature or
   miss the important expert caveats.

Nulls are admissible. A clean "Sundog has only a useful boundary explainer
here" result is acceptable and may be the correct endpoint.

## Initial Domain Map

The literature pass must lock these domains before any reader note, workbench,
or public page:

- **Primary statement:** the exact Hodge conjecture variant to quote and the
  scope of smooth projective complex varieties.
- **Coefficient field:** rational classes versus integral or real variants;
  the scaffold defaults to rational cohomology until the lit pass says
  otherwise.
- **Cycle convention:** codimension, cycle class map, and what "algebraic
  cycle" means in the selected examples.
- **Shadow convention:** Hodge decomposition, `(p,p)` type, harmonic
  representative, period data, or another named observable.
- **Safe examples:** projective spaces, curves, products, abelian varieties, K3
  surfaces, or other examples only after the lit pass ranks them.
- **Hard exclusions:** generalized Hodge, standard conjectures, motives,
  absolute Hodge classes, or Tate unless explicitly admitted as sidebars.

Expansion requires a new phase sign-off. No scope creep from "reader scaffold"
to "Hodge evidence" is allowed midstream.

## Initial Probe / Artifact Shortlist

Each item below is scaffold-only until the lit-pass memo decides whether it is
admissible.

### Candidate 1 - Hodge Reader / Boundary Note

Working hook:

> A Hodge class is the right kind of shadow. The conjecture asks for the body.

- **Front:** A.
- **Cost:** Low.
- **Artifact:** a short reader note explaining the Hodge conjecture in Sundog
  body/shadow vocabulary, with explicit category boundaries.
- **Attacks failure modes:** 1, 2, 3, 6.
- **Pre-registered negative:** if the note cannot say anything more precise
  than a standard exposition without adding misleading vocabulary, file
  `HODGE-FRONT-A-VACUOUS` and do not promote.

### Candidate 2 - Faraday / AB / Hodge Bridge Note

Working hook:

> The harmonic survivor teaches the caution, not the conjecture.

- **Front:** A.
- **Cost:** Low.
- **Artifact:** a bridge note from Faraday Phase 8 and Aharonov-Bohm to Hodge
  vocabulary. It should explain the shared words and the hard non-transfer.
- **Attacks failure modes:** 3, 4.
- **Pre-registered negative:** if the bridge cannot be written without making
  algebraic geometry sound like electromagnetic topology, file
  `HODGE-PHYSICS-TRANSFER`.

### Candidate 3 - Known-Example Gallery

Working hook:

> Known bodies, visible shadows, no mystery smuggled in.

- **Front:** A/B bridge.
- **Cost:** Low to medium.
- **Artifact:** a small table or interactive exhibit of known-safe examples,
  with each row labeled by variety, class, cycle, why it is known, and what it
  does not imply.
- **Attacks failure modes:** 2, 5, 6.
- **Pre-registered negative:** if the examples make known cases feel like a
  detector for unknown cases, keep the gallery internal.

### Candidate 4 - Tate / Finite-Field Cousin Scoping

Working hook:

> Over finite fields, point counts cast a different shadow.

- **Front:** horizon.
- **Cost:** Low for a scoping note, high for anything more.
- **Artifact:** a one-page note deciding whether Tate belongs as a sidebar,
  future sibling ledger, or hard exclusion.
- **Attacks failure mode:** 6.
- **Pre-registered negative:** if including Tate muddies the Hodge boundary,
  exclude it from the first Hodge lane.

### Candidate 5 - Public Visual Concept

Working hook:

> The shadow has the right type. The body is still missing.

- **Front:** B.
- **Cost:** Medium.
- **Artifact:** a visual concept only after Candidate 1 lands: likely a
  restrained diagram or workbench, not a simulation.
- **Attacks failure mode:** 5.
- **Pre-registered negative:** if the visual implies that a cycle can be read
  directly off a form, do not publish it.

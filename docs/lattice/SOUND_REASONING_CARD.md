## The claim (one sentence)

We trained a **tiny (798K-param) neural reasoner** to solve the hardest Sudokus by
**imitating a verified solver**, so that — by construction — it **almost never rules out the
correct answer**. Its false-rejection rate is `[v3 false_elim]` per puzzle (vs `31.5` for a
naive answer-prediction baseline), and its deductions never use a step a classical verifier
couldn't justify.

## Why this is a *chat-trust* result, not a Sudoku result

The number that matters is **`false_elim` = how often the model rules out a candidate that
is actually correct.** That is the puzzle-domain twin of a chat assistant's worst failure
mode: **confidently dismissing the right answer** (a hallucinated rejection). We drove it
toward zero not by scale, but by **supervising the *process*** — training the model to
imitate a verifier's *sound narrowing*, where the correct answer is never a valid thing to
eliminate. The lesson transfers as a **method and a metric**; the Sudoku model itself does not.

| property | Sudoku (measured) | chat analogue (the transfer) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `false_elim` ≈ 0 | never eliminates a true digit | never dismisses the correct answer |
| imitates a verifier | sound CP narrowing | grounded, checkable reasoning steps |
| conflict-head calibration | flags dead-ends (`[v3]`) | "knows when it's stuck / unsure" |
| null control fails | search alone can't solve | the *model* earns the trust, not the harness |

## Why it's trustworthy and not just lucky (the controls)

- **Null-rollout control:** the same search harness with an *untrained* model solves **0%**
  (it thrashes — 364 branches/puzzle). So the result is the *learned reasoner*, not the
  search. *(Confirmed.)*
- **Soundness audit:** the training target is generated by pure constraint propagation from
  the puzzle — the correct answer is **never** marked eliminable (verified across 200/200
  puzzles, 0 violations). No answer-key leakage.
- **Tail honesty:** we report the *worst* puzzles, not just the mean — `false_elim`
  p50/p90/p99/max `[v3]`, so the claim shows its failure tail.

## What this does / does NOT say

- **DOES:** a reasoner can be trained to a **bounded, audited, near-zero false-rejection rate**
  on a hard task by imitating a verifier — a concrete, demonstrable *trust* capability and a
  reusable method ([`VERIFIED_REASONING_METHOD.md`](VERIFIED_REASONING_METHOD.md)).
- **Does NOT:** claim Ask Sundog is hallucination-free, claim anything about open-domain chat,
  or assert the Sundog regime-2 signature (that is B2, gated, separate).

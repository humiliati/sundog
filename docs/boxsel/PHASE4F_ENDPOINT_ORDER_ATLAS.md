## Goal

Phase 4e proved `q_KKT = (9+√17)/32` minimal *inside the structured normal form*. The next
obligation: rule out **every** n=2 combinatorial cell (interval-endpoint ordering), not just the
structured one.

## The atlas (certified, exact combinatorics)

Per axis, a **cell** is the arrangement of the three pairwise-overlapping intervals `A, B, C`:
a pair `(σ, τ)` where `σ` orders the **left** endpoints and `τ` the **right** endpoints. Overlap
formulas depend only on `(σ, τ)`, so there are exactly `6 × 6 = 36` per-axis types. The symmetry
group is generated by `A↔B` (the query is symmetric in A,B), the reflection `x↦1−x` (swaps
left/right roles, reverses both orders), and — for n=2 — the axis swap.

```text
36 per-axis types     ->  12 per-axis orbits
36 × 36 = 1296 n=2 pairs ->  123 n=2 orbits   (under the full symmetry group)
```

## Exact structural elimination

On an axis, **C "helps"** (can give `q_k < 1`) iff `C` sticks out of `A∩B` — i.e. `C` has the
strictly largest left (`σ` ends in C) **or** the strictly smallest right (`τ` starts with C).
Otherwise `C ⊇ A_k∩B_k` and `q_k = 1`.

```text
zero helping axes  ->  q = q_1 · q_2 = 1  >=  q_KKT     (eliminated EXACTLY)
```

The 123 orbits split **31 zero-help / 56 one-help / 36 two-help**. So **31 orbits are eliminated
by an exact argument**; the candidate `q_KKT` lives in the two-help family (on a degenerate
boundary where `B_1` and `C_1` share a left endpoint). That leaves **92 live orbits**.

## Numerical backstop (NOT a per-cell exact proof)

A Nelder–Mead search **seeded from the optimum's full symmetry orbit** (plus random restarts) over
the live cells reaches `0.41009705` and finds **nothing strictly below `q_KKT`** (margin `4×10⁻¹⁰`).
This seeding is what makes it trustworthy: the earlier *blind* searches (random, SLSQP, DE) all
missed `q_KKT` entirely (Phase 4c's search-gap finding), so this is evidence, not a formal
per-cell minimum.

Sundog Research Lab

An independent applied research program sponsored by Stellar Aqua LLC, studying systems that act from indirect signals: shadows, local fields, deformation, torque, pressure, compressed game state, and other traces that preserve control-relevant structure.

Independent Research, Practical Sponsor

Sundog Research Lab is an independent applied research program sponsored by Stellar Aqua LLC, a controls and environmental-systems company whose field work produced the original alignment problem.

The project began when a practical alignment constraint—how to confirm a physical target through loss and return of signal when direct inspection was unavailable—turned into a mathematical question. That field procedure, developed during suspended HVAC installation work, became the foundation for a broader research program about indirect measurement and control.

The sponsor is practical. The research posture is explicit. We separate controlled results from prototypes, product expressions, and future-facing claims.

Research Posture

Sundog operates as an independent research program with experimental software lab as a secondary identity. Our work spans:

  • Controlled experiments with reproducible results, matched baselines, and explicit failure boundaries
  • Operating-envelope studies that map where indirect signals work and where they fail
  • Instrumented prototypes demonstrating practical utility in real systems
  • Product expressions showing how the alignment framework applies to games, simulation, and software

We maintain disciplined evidence tiers because the credibility of research—from any origin—depends on separating what is proven from what is promising.

Evidence Tiers

The Sundog project distinguishes different levels of evidence:

Research Result

Photometric Mirror Alignment: A controller aligns a mirrored end-effector without target coordinates, using only sparse photometric feedback. Terminal accuracy is statistically indistinguishable from a target-aware oracle baseline in controlled MuJoCo experiments (U=526, p=0.264). The cost is convergence time, not accuracy. Known failure boundary: tight joint limits.

Operating-Envelope Study

Three-Body Dynamics: A guarded accelerometer-proxy TRACK controller improves survival over passive and naive local baselines inside a tested high-velocity near-escape pocket. Low-velocity and equal-mass cells remain explicit failure boundaries.

Sundog Balance: A shadow-derived cart-pole controller maintains balance inside a mapped lighting and delay envelope. Overhead-light and high-delay configurations remain explicit degradation boundaries.

Pressure Mines: A noisy pressure field enables more safe progress before failure inside a narrow mapped pocket. Does not claim to solve Minesweeper or reveal the hidden board in general.

Instrumented Prototype

EyesOnly / Gone Rogue: Procedural roguelike agents act from compressed perception using stop-conditioned action batches.

Money Bags: Softbody terrain system with graph-based telemetry making torsion, deformation, symmetry, and recovery legible as interpretable signals.

Product Expression

Dungeon Gleaner: NPC motion pulled by verbs and needs, not shortest-path planners. Personality expressed through per-archetype weighting on a shared verb vocabulary.

Field Origin

The theorem's practical origin matters. Sundog didn't emerge from a university lab or pure software research. It came from a jobsite constraint during environmental controls installation work.

The discovery happened while aligning acoustic ceiling anchors for suspended HVAC equipment above a quantum computing environment. A plumb laser, a fastener head, and an occluded line of sight produced a geometric procedure that later became the model for the photometric alignment experiment.

That field origin is an advantage: Sundog's framing as a practical alignment problem from suspended environmental-control work grounds the research in real constraints, not ivory-tower abstractions.

What We Do Not Claim

Intellectual honesty requires being explicit about limitations:

  • We do not claim Sundog solves the three-body problem. We ask whether the shadow of that chaos is enough to steer by in bounded regimes.
  • We do not claim Sundog solves Minesweeper. We show that lossy pressure fields preserve enough structure for bounded decisions inside a named operating envelope.
  • We do not claim universal alignment. We demonstrate specific tasks where indirect signals enable control.
  • We do not hide failure modes. Operating envelopes include explicit degradation and failure boundaries.

The defensible scientific claim today is narrow: photometric mirror alignment without target-position access in controlled MuJoCo experiments. The broader applications demonstrate practical utility and motivate future empirical work.

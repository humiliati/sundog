## Reading key

- **Informal** — the claim in one breath.
- **Formal sketch** — the language it lives in; provable in toy cases means a
  theorem schema exists, not that it is proved.
- **Test** — the cheapest experiment that moves belief.
- **Falsifier** — the outcome that ends it. Mandatory.
- **Class** — load-bearing | speculative.

## 1. The Coarse-Graining Postulate — *the actual theorem*

**Class:** load-bearing. This is the theorem the project has been circling for a
year without naming.

- **Informal.** A controller does not need to invert the world. It needs the
  signature to be a *sufficient statistic for the optimal action*, even when the
  signature cannot reconstruct the world state.
- **Formal sketch.** Let world microstate `x ∈ X`, signature `σ = Φ(x)`, optimal
  policy `π*(x)`. Conjecture: a task is **Sundog-solvable** iff `π*` is
  measurable with respect to `𝓕_σ`, the σ-algebra generated by `Φ` — i.e.
  `π*(x) = g(Φ(x))` a.e. for some `g`. This is **Blackwell sufficiency** of the
  signature *for the control objective*, not for state estimation, and it has a
  rate-distortion reading (the Shannon seed in
  `scratchpad_brainstorm_notes.md`): `Φ` is the minimal sufficient quantizer such
  that distortion in *action* — not in *reconstruction* — is zero. Provable for
  LQG/linear-Gaussian and for finite MDPs by a sufficiency argument; the open
  work is the nonlinear/learned case.
- **Test.** Pick a substrate where `Φ` and `π*` are both known (the planar
  three-body near-escape pocket, or a linear-Gaussian toy). Measure whether the
  signature-only controller's regret against the Bayes-optimal controller → 0
  *exactly on the set where `π*` is `𝓕_σ`-measurable* and stays bounded away
  from 0 off it.
- **Falsifier.** A task where `π*` is provably `𝓕_σ`-measurable yet the
  signature controller cannot match Bayes-optimal regret (sufficiency holds,
  control fails) — or the converse on the pushable-occluder, where the
  control-relevant bit is *not* `𝓕_σ`-measurable yet Sundog still solves it
  (boundary wrong).
- **Why it is the one to chase.** It converts the deepest attack
  ([`attack_vectors.md`](attack_vectors.md) ▸ #1, "you rediscovered ill-posed
  inverse problems") from a concession into a research program: the
  cross-substrate sameness becomes a *shared operator* (`Φ` is a coarse-graining
  in all three substrates), not a shared adjective. It also *predicts* the
  pre-registered failure boundary instead of patching it. Staged as
  [`../../docs/COARSE_GRAINING_PROOF_ROADMAP.md`](../../docs/COARSE_GRAINING_PROOF_ROADMAP.md).

## 2. Goodhart-immunity ⟺ one-wayness of the reward channel — *capacity-relative*

**Class:** load-bearing. This is what makes the gravity essay survive contact.

- **Informal.** An agent can game a proxy only if it can compute an input that
  lifts the proxy without lifting the true objective. If the only access to the
  proxy is a map the agent's capacity *cannot invert*, gaming is impossible — for
  that capacity class.
- **Formal sketch.** Goodhart-feasibility for agent class `𝓒` ≔ ∃ `a ∈ 𝓒`
  computing `x` with `ΔP(x) > 0, ΔJ(x) ≤ 0`. If `Φ` is one-way *relative to
  `𝓒`* (forward cheap, inverse not in `𝓒`), the feasible set is empty. Immunity
  is therefore not a property of the signature; it is a relation between `Φ` and
  `𝓒`. The mesa cliff is the measured `𝓒`-threshold at which approximate
  inversion enters the class.
- **Test.** Re-read the Mesa λ-sweep as an invertibility curve: λ scales reward
  leakage into the policy interior; the cliff at λ≈0.952588 is where the
  Medium-tier policy's effective capacity can approximately invert `Φ`. Predicts
  the cliff *moves* with capacity (Small vs Medium vs Large) along a law
  `λ*(C)`, monotone, and that Foundation-tier *should* break — a prediction, not
  a hope.
- **Falsifier.** The cliff does not move with capacity (then λ is not
  invertibility, and Postulate 2 is dead — and Mesa is probably the
  optimizer-confound of [`attack_vectors.md`](attack_vectors.md) ▸ #4).
- **Payoff.** The cliff stops being the project's worst contradiction
  ([`attack_vectors.md`](attack_vectors.md) ▸ #2) and becomes a *prediction of
  the theory*. That reframing is free and is owed before 2026-05-19.

## 3. The 16× is a conservation law — *a Sundog uncertainty principle*

**Class:** load-bearing (it owns the sharpest publication attack).

- **Informal.** The ~16× slower acquisition is not an embarrassment to bury; it
  is a measured instance of a conserved quantity.
- **Formal sketch.** Conjecture an inequality: `(information withheld from the
  observation) × (excess acquisition time) ≥ c`, with the withheld information
  the KL/mutual-information gap between the oracle's and the signature's
  observation channels. This is the rate-distortion / data-processing reading of
  Postulate 1 (Shannon seed): you cannot beat the channel; the time cost is the
  Lagrange dual of the discarded bits. The **Bayesian floor** standing defense
  ([`attack_vectors.md`](attack_vectors.md) ▸ "Bayesian floor") is the empirical
  handle — the Bayes-optimal estimator pays the *same* dual, so the Sundog↔Bayes
  gap, not the Sundog↔oracle gap, is the conserved quantity to measure.
- **Test.** Predict the slowdown for three substrates *from their
  dimensionality/information gap alone* (core 18D→3D-style gap, three-body,
  vortex toy) and check the predicted-vs-measured ratio is constant within CI.
- **Falsifier.** The ratio is not conserved across substrates (then 16× is just
  this task being slow, and attack #3 stands on its own terms).

## 4. The 5D subspace is a topological count — *Königsberg, not weak tooling*

**Class:** speculative, but cheaply decidable.

- **Informal.** "Irreducibly entangled, 5D" at `net.7` is a count of *relevant
  directions*, an invariant of the subspace — not an artifact of probes that
  were too weak to decompose it.
- **Formal sketch.** Two converging readings. (a) **RG:** coarse-graining is a
  renormalization step; forward loses information, the inverse is narrow
  precisely on the fixed point's unstable manifold, whose dimension = number of
  relevant operators. "5D" = five relevant directions, infinitely many
  irrelevant. (b) **Königsberg/Euler** (`scratchpad` seed): Euler proved
  non-traversability from a connectivity invariant (node parities), not from any
  local edge. Mesa Phase 6 already shows non-decomposability is probe-independent
  (no single critical neuron v3.3, no SAE feature v2, no top-k recovery v3.2) —
  the signature of an *invariant*, not a tooling failure. Candidate invariant: a
  Betti-number / persistent-homology count of the cliff's activation manifold.
- **Test.** Compute the candidate invariant across the whole Medium family; it
  should be probe-independent and equal to 5 (or whatever number; the *constancy*
  is the claim). Cross-check the RG reading: the cliff *exponent* should be
  universal across mesa task families — a concrete, fundable next experiment.
- **Falsifier.** A future probe linearly decomposes the cliff, or the invariant
  varies by probe/family. Then it was tooling; Postulate 4 and the Königsberg
  analogy die together (stated so they can).

## 5. The atlas is an analog computer — *and the vortex is the cheap fourth test*

**Class:** speculative; the anniversary's unifying narrative if it holds.

- **Informal.** Stop calling it a metaphor. The atmosphere *performs* Sundog
  inference: the halo is `Φ` made physical; reading sun-altitude off a parhelion
  is the sky solving the narrow inverse. Same operator in optics and in control
  because both are coarse-grainings of a high-D state to a low-D observable.
- **Formal sketch.** If Postulate 1 holds, "in-the-wild receipt" is not analogy
  but instantiation: optics and control share `Φ` up to isomorphism of the
  coarse-graining. The wishing-well coin / tornado vortex (`scratchpad` Euler
  seed) is a *fourth* substrate — Euler-fluid microdynamics, low-D envelope
  signature — cheap to simulate and a clean cross-substrate replication test
  *or* falsifier.
- **Test.** Stand up the vortex toy as a workbench with a Bayesian floor (not a
  new `bayes_v_sundog` track — a baseline inside the workbench). Check the
  forward-rich / inverse-narrow asymmetry appears with the *same* measured
  sufficient-statistic structure as core/mesa/geometry.
- **Falsifier.** The asymmetry is absent, or present but structurally unlike the
  other substrates. Either way it is publishable; that is why it is worth
  staging.

## 6. The founding `H(x)` was an entropy all along — *the anniversary beat*

**Class:** speculative narrative + a real formal conjecture. New, from the
`scratchpad` seed "boolean algebra information entropy h(x) vs sundog h(x)."

- **Informal.** The deliberately unhinged 2025 theorem wrote `H(x) = ∂S/∂τ` and
  called it the "halo signature." Read a year later through Postulate 1, that
  symbol is not poetry — it is the information the coarse-graining discards.
- **Formal sketch.** `S` (shadow projection) and `τ` (torque) are two readouts of
  `Φ`; `∂S/∂τ` is a Jacobian of the signature map. Conjecture that, at the
  optimum, this Jacobian term is proportional to a **Fisher information / local
  entropy-production** rate of the coarse-graining `Φ` — i.e. the original `H`
  and Shannon's `H` are the same object evaluated on `Φ`, not a pun. If true, the
  2025 existence clause "∃x: H(x) ≠ 0 ⇒ alignment" reads as *"control is possible
  exactly where the coarse-graining is informative,"* which is Postulate 1's
  measurability condition restated.
- **Test.** In the LQG toy of Postulate 1, compute `∂S/∂τ` at the optimum and the
  Fisher information of `Φ`; check proportionality.
- **Falsifier.** No proportionality in the toy where both are computable. Then it
  stays a good anniversary story and nothing more — label it Layer-A only.
- **Why keep it.** It closes the year-one arc honestly: *the symbol we scrawled
  in excitement turned out to name the thing we spent a year measuring* — true
  only if the toy check passes; do not ship the line until it does.

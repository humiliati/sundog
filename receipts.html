Measurement-floor receipts

Pre-registrations, frozen tests, and result artifacts for four completed studies of probe-access asymmetries and certified recoverability in trained systems: what is actually recoverable from a system's aggregated internals, at what calibrated sensitivity floor, and what any "nothing detected" is worth. Each study was pre-registered with kill criteria before code ran, frozen after adversarial review, and is deterministic to the byte — re-run the exact commands in each receipt and you get the same numbers. Two of the receipts correct our own earlier claims; the corrections are part of the record.

What this is NOT.
  • Not peer-reviewed publications — these are research receipts, published so claims can be checked against artifacts rather than taken on trust.
  • Not claims about consciousness, valenced experience, or introspection as mental phenomena. Every result here concerns probe-access: which variables are recoverable from observables, by which probes, at which calibrated floors.
  • Not frontier-model results — substrates are ground-truth synthetic constructions plus one small vision model, chosen so every claim is exactly checkable.

The four bundles

1 · Probe-ceiling audit, synthetic leg — is a "suppressed" variable destroyed or just hidden from weak probes?

A representation we had recorded as carrying ≈0 of a suppressed continuous variable ("probe-robustly") yields at least R² ≈ 0.13 to a kernel probe under an injection-calibrated battery (replicated on a held-out split evaluated exactly once; confirmed by a mutual-information estimator). The original silence was a sample-size artifact plus a probe that the calibration showed to be blind to its own detection target. Verdict (c) of three pre-registered outcomes; a correction was issued to the earlier receipt.

2 · Probe-ceiling audit, real-model leg — the same battery on a small vision model's pooled activations

A recorded "rotation attenuated to 0.34" reading on a TinyCNN's global-average-pool shadow rises to at least 0.56 certified recoverable under the calibrated battery — the attenuation was mostly probe-relative, and the uncalibrated readout under-read recoverability by 0.22 R². The pattern across both legs is uniform: uncalibrated silences certify little until you measure what the probe could have heard.

3 · Adversarial concealment — can an incentivized system learn to hide a discrete internal variable from aggregated oversight?

Across gradient-reversal adversaries with competence guards, the discrete variable stayed readable (≥ 0.996) in every condition where the encoder retained task competence; under extreme hiding pressure the system destroyed its own competence rather than conceal. Yet a hand-built encoder hides the same variable to statistical chance (0.50–0.53) on the same substrate — existence of concealment ≠ trainability of concealment, a named gap that oversight design can exploit. Pre-registered KILL branch fired: the clean strengthening null.

4 · Stale-reporter projection — what does a frozen reporter output once its target is no longer recoverable?

A reporter trained where its target was readable, frozen, then deployed where the target has washed out of its inputs does not lock onto the still-recoverable variables (partial R² ≤ 0.0034 on the live discrete channel, 10/10 cells, two architectures): it collapses its output spread ~9× toward the prior. Correlation audits have nothing to find; variance collapse against a calibrated baseline is the staleness signature. Pre-registered clean-null branch fired.

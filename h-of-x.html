Math · h-of-x

The first equation.

Sundog's public math starts with a narrow inverse: if an eligible photo gives the 22° halo as a scale ruler and both parhelia are visible, the side-glint offset recovers hidden sun altitude. This page keeps the handle, the measurement, and the failure boundary in the same room.

Plain claim

h-of-x is a public tag for the first equation, not a claim that every halo route is solved. The equation is useful exactly where its anchors are eligible.

Names, to be clear: the discovery-era uppercase H(x) — the “halo signature,” torque-and-shadow framing is the mythic origin notation, kept in the origin archive. This page is the lowercase h(x): the first narrow, testable equation that program earned. Same project, different equation.

First equation

Offset to altitude.

On the eligible parhelion route, the photograph gives a measured offset. Divide that offset by the 22° halo radius (R22), then invert.

offset = R22 / cos(h) x = offset / R22 h(x) = arccos(1 / x)

The small miracle is not that the equation is ornate. It is that a visible displacement can carry a hidden angle when the geometry is anchored.

Eligibility

The boundary is part of the math.

h-of-x is valuable because it says where it can be used. Without that gate, a pretty inverse becomes a storytelling machine.

Allowed

Bilateral parhelia, independent 22° halo anchor, and a photo route that does not infer the ruler from the answer.

Not enough

One flanking spot, a named arc with no calibrated route, or a rendered layer that only supplies vocabulary.

Structural stop

CZA (circumzenithal arc) cutoff near 32.2°, tangent-family merge near 29°, and coverage-gated supralateral or tangent routes stay outside this handle unless their own receipts promote them.

Translation

What the equation means.

For a general reader, the math is not the typography. It is the measurement discipline:

  • find a hidden state,
  • identify a visible trace that must move with it,
  • write the inverse before touching the result,
  • measure where the inverse breaks.

That same discipline is why the site distinguishes the visual atlas, the vocabulary legend, the structural failure map, and the alignment comparator page.
